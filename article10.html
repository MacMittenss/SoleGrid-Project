New Balance 990 - Dad Shoe Turned Icon

Written By SoleGrid Team | March 2025
Tech

When the **New Balance 990** first hit the market in **1982**, it was an instant game-changer. Designed as a **premium running shoe**, the 990 was one of the first sneakers to feature cutting-edge materials like **ENCAP cushioning**, which provided superior support and durability. Its high price tag of $100 was unheard of at the time, making it a luxury sneaker for serious runners.

Despite its performance-driven origins, the 990 gradually became a **streetwear staple**, gaining popularity among dads, fashion enthusiasts, and celebrities alike. Its **chunky silhouette and understated colorways** fit perfectly into the "dad shoe" trend, which later took over the fashion world. Luxury designers and streetwear brands began embracing the aesthetic, pushing the 990 into high fashion.

One of the reasons for the **New Balance 990’s enduring appeal** is its **Made in the USA craftsmanship**. Unlike mass-produced sneakers, the 990 series continues to be manufactured in the United States, maintaining a reputation for quality, comfort, and durability. Over the years, multiple iterations of the 990—such as the **990v2, 990v3, 990v4, and 990v5**—have refined the classic design while keeping its core identity intact.

Today, the New Balance 990 is a **symbol of authenticity and effortless style**. Whether worn by runners, celebrities, or streetwear enthusiasts, the 990 has proven that a sneaker can be both functional and fashionable. In an era of hype-driven releases, the 990 stands strong as a **timeless classic** that continues to shape sneaker culture.
