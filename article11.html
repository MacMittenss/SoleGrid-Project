Puma Suede - Hip-Hop’s Favorite Sneaker

Written By SoleGrid Team | March 2025
Entertainment

Few sneakers have had a cultural impact as deep as the **Puma Suede**. First introduced in **1968**, the Suede was originally designed as a basketball shoe but quickly became a symbol of street style and self-expression. Its sleek **suede upper** and durable rubber sole made it stand out from traditional leather sneakers of the time.

One of the defining moments for the Puma Suede came in **1968**, when Olympic athlete **Tommie Smith** raised his fist in a Black Power salute while standing on the medal podium—his Puma Suedes placed beside him. This moment solidified the sneaker’s connection to activism and counterculture.

By the **1980s**, the Puma Suede had found a new home in hip-hop and breakdancing culture. Groups like the **Rock Steady Crew** made them an essential part of b-boy fashion, as their flexibility and grip were perfect for dance battles. The Suede was also embraced by rap legends like **Grandmaster Flash**, reinforcing its place in the music industry.

Over the decades, the Puma Suede has been reimagined in countless **collaborations with brands, musicians, and artists**, including partnerships with **FUBU, Bobbito Garcia, and even Ferrari**. Whether on the feet of a breakdancer, a rapper, or a fashion enthusiast, the Puma Suede has remained a timeless staple in urban culture.
